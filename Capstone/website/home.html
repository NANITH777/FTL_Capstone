SDG 2 & SDG 13

Impacts of climate change on food security, Agriculture.

As the world grapples with the pressing challenges of climate change, the implications for food security are profound and far-reaching. Our project leverages machine learning to analyze and predict critical factors influencing food security, specifically focusing on crop yield, prevalence of undernourishment, and climate yield. By understanding these dynamics, we aim to contribute valuable insights to the global discourse on achieving Zero Hunger (SDG 2) while addressing the urgent need for climate Action (SDG 13). Our findings not only highlight the vulnerabilities of agricultural systems but also underscore the necessity of sustainable practices to ensure food security for future generations. Join us on this journey to explore the intersection of climate change and food security, as we strive to foster resilience in our food systems.

Crop Yield

climate change significantly impacts agriculture, threatening global food security by affecting crop yields through extreme weather, altered rainfall, and increased pests. Crop Yield uses data-driven models, including machine learning, to forecast harvest outcomes based on environmental conditions, weather data, soil quality, and agricultural practices.

Accurate predictions help farmers and policymakers make informed decisions on planting, resource management, and risk mitigation, enhancing resilience, optimizing agricultural productivity, and ensuring food security in a changing climate.

Undernourishment Prediction

Undernourishment remains a critical global challenge, exacerbated by climate change's impact on food production and distribution. As changing weather patterns, extreme events, and resource constraints threaten agricultural outputs, vulnerable populations face increased risks of food insecurity and inadequate nutrition.

Undernourishment Prediction leverages data analytics, machine learning, and socio-economic indicators to forecast areas and populations at risk of hunger.

Emissions

Agriculture is both a victim and a contributor to climate change, responsible for significant greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions, including carbon dioxide (CO₂), methane (CH₄), and nitrous oxide (N₂O). Emissions from agricultural activities, such as livestock digestion, rice paddies, deforestation, and the use of synthetic fertilizers, contribute to global warming, exacerbating climate change.

Reducing agricultural emissions is essential for mitigating climate change. Key strategies include sustainable farming, better livestock management, optimized fertilizer use, and enhanced carbon sequestration through agroforestry.
